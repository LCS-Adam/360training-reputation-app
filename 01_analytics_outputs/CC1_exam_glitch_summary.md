# CC1 — Exam-Glitch Crosswalk Summary

**Sources:** `360training_exam_glitches.csv` (n=211, a pre-filtered exam/test-glitch subset) cross-referenced against the in-corpus `exam_test_glitch` theme (n=458 across the full 10,601) and overall negative volume, by quarter.
**Companion data:** `CC1_exam_glitch_crosswalk.csv`.

## Headline: exam glitches are a *persistent baseline irritant*, not the trigger of the 2024 sentiment break
Glitch signal is roughly flat across the decline, not spiking with it:

| | 2023 quarters | 2024 quarters | 2025 quarters |
|---|---|---|---|
| Glitch-CSV reviews/qtr | 5–10 | 3–12 | 6–12 |
| Corpus glitch-theme/qtr | 11–26 | 11–22 | 10–20 |

There is **no glitch surge aligned to the March-2024 or late-2024 inflections.** The platform reliability complaint is chronic and steady, present before, during, and after the decline.

## Severity is high even though volume is flat
- **75.4% of the exam-glitch reviews are 1–2★**; the corpus glitch theme runs 60.7% negative.
- These are visceral, high-effort-loss complaints ("kicked me out 3 times when I was incredibly deep, had to restart"). Individually damaging, collectively steady.

## Refinement of the inherited narrative (value-add)
The source memos lean on exam/test glitches as a central driver of the 2024 collapse. The timing data says: **glitches explain chronic dissatisfaction, but the 2024 *deterioration* is better explained by the growth themes — billing/refund, scam/fraud, access/expiration, and support-service** (see special-topic + change-point summaries), which rise into the decline window while glitches stay flat. Keep exam glitches as a standing product-reliability liability, but don't over-attribute the inflection to them.

**Confidence:** medium-high. The CSV is a pre-filtered subset (its absolute level depends on the upstream filter), so the *flatness* is read together with the independently-tagged corpus theme, which agrees.
